# about.lede

# about.p1
I believe that only problems which can be clearly defined and verified can truly be solved. Evaluation is not just a business metric, but the compass of AI — it decides where we head, and how we judge whether we are making progress.

# about.p2
I also believe that data is to AI what lived experience is to a person — intelligence does not emerge from a junk pile; high-quality data determines high-quality intelligence. My work is to abstract and sample real, complex user tasks into reproducible evaluations that observe and define the capability frontier, then to construct high-quality training data that pushes it outward.

# research.heading
Research

# r1.h
Where is the boundary of the current paradigm of intelligence?

# r1.p
What problems can the current paradigm never truly solve? And how can a model evolve itself? This is my newest — and biggest — interest; for now I have only surveys and leads (early exploration: EvoTrainer).

# r2.h
How do we evaluate agents in real production environments?

# r2.p
Real-world software engineering is far more than “functional correctness.” I focus on agents’ long-horizon ability in real codebases, on non-functional metrics such as architectural and stylistic consistency, readability, and maintainability — and on extending this to vision/front-end and to broader domains such as medicine, law, and finance.

# r3.h
What are the mechanisms and limits of multi-agent collaboration?

# r3.p
When multiple agents collaborate, how should communication and reasoning be organized? Does multi-agent have its own “Mythical Man-Month”? I study communication and coordination in multi-agent reasoning.
