# about.lede

# about.p1
I believe that only problems which can be clearly defined and verified can truly be solved. Evaluation is not just a business metric, but the compass of AI — it decides where we head, and how we judge whether we are making progress.

# about.p2
I also believe that data is to AI what lived experience is to a person — intelligence does not emerge from a junk pile; high-quality data determines high-quality intelligence. My work is to abstract and sample real, complex user tasks into reproducible evaluations that observe and define the capability frontier, then to construct high-quality training data that pushes it outward.

# r2.p
Real-world software engineering is far more than "functional correctness." I focus on agents' long-horizon ability in real codebases, on non-functional metrics such as architectural and stylistic consistency, readability, and maintainability — and on extending this to vision/front-end and to broader domains such as medicine, law, and finance.

# work.full
Full publication list ↗

# w1.d
The first benchmark to evaluate agents' ability to maintain real codebases using continuous-integration signal; it exposes how often agents regress across iterative rounds. (co-first author)

# w2.d
Streaming communication in multi-agent reasoning, so coordination cost does not erase the benefit of more agents. (co-corresponding author)

# w3.d
Technical report on building the ROME model within an open agentic learning ecosystem. I served as a core contributor, responsible for evaluation and annotation data.

# w4.d
A next-generation agent benchmark grounded in real terminal environments, with systematic upgrades in scale and contamination control. (project lead)

# w5.d
A benchmark targeting tool-use capabilities of the Qwen model family, covering multi-domain and multi-turn tool-use scenarios. (project lead)

# w6.d
Systematic verification and structured revision of Humanity's Last Exam to make frontier-capability evaluation more trustworthy. (core contributor)

# w7.d
A code-agent benchmark for the Qoder product, used to continuously track and regress product capability. (core contributor)
