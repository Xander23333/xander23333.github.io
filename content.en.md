# about.p1
I believe that only problems which can be clearly defined and verified can truly be solved. Evaluation is therefore not just a business metric, but the compass of research — it decides where we head, and how we judge whether we are making progress.

# research.heading
Research

# r1.h
How do we scientifically measure capability — and the boundary of the current paradigm?

# r1.p
How do we tell whether a benchmark or a dataset is truly high-quality? Where is the ceiling of the current training paradigm, and can it be measured quantitatively? Getting evaluation right matters more than chasing single metrics.

# r2.h
How do we evaluate software engineering in real production environments?

# r2.p
Real-world software engineering is far more than “functional correctness.” I focus on agents’ long-horizon ability in real codebases, and on non-functional metrics such as architectural and stylistic consistency, readability, and maintainability.

# r3.h
What are the mechanisms and limits of multi-agent collaboration?

# r3.p
When multiple agents collaborate, how should communication and reasoning be organized? Does multi-agent have its own “Mythical Man-Month”? I study communication and coordination in multi-agent reasoning.
